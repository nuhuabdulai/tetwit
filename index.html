About TeTWIT

Bridging the gender gap in educational technology

The Problem

Addressing Gender Disparities in ICT

The formation of Teacher Trainee Women in Information Technology (TeTWIT) Clubs in Colleges of Education in Ghana is a timely and strategic intervention aimed at addressing persistent gender disparities in the acquisition, use, and leadership of information and communication technology (ICT) within teacher education.

Current Challenges

No structured women-focused ICT clubs exist across Ghana's Colleges of Education
Limited peer support networks for female trainees
Low confidence in ICT-related academic spaces
Reduced visibility of female teacher trainees in technology
Cultural expectations and gender stereotypes discourage engagement
Female trainees underrepresented in advanced ICT use and innovation

Our Solution

TeTWIT Clubs will provide a structured, supportive, and collaborative environment where female teacher trainees can develop practical ICT skills, share experiences, and build confidence in using digital tools for teaching, assessment, research, and professional practice.

Hands-on activities and peer learning
Workshops and guided projects
Low-risk, encouraging environment
Tailored to women's specific needs

Teacher Education Focus

Today's trainees will shape the attitudes and competencies of future generations of learners, especially girls in basic schools.

Mentorship Platform

Exposing members to female role models in educational technology, ICT facilitation, and digital entrepreneurship.

Long-term Impact

Producing digitally confident female teachers who can serve as ICT champions, coordinators, and innovators.

Club Constitution

Governing principles and operational guidelines of TeTWIT

"Empower Her to Code the Future."

— Official TeTWIT Motto

Article 1

Name of the Club

The name of the organisation shall be Teacher Trainee Women in Information Technology (TeTWIT).

Article 3

Vision & Mission

Vision: To be the leading national platform that empowers teacher trainee women in Ghana with ICT skills and leadership in digital education.

Mission: To create and support a network of digitally literate and tech-savvy female educators who will transform education and bridge gender gaps in technology.

Article 4

Aims & Objectives

  • Enhance digital literacy among teacher trainee women
  • Promote career development in IT through training and mentorship
  • Engage in projects and events that empower women in tech
  • Foster partnerships with stakeholders in education and technology
  • Advocate for inclusive and gender-responsive technology policies
Article 5

Membership

Eligibility: Open to all female teacher trainees in any accredited College of Education in Ghana.

Associate Membership: Open to male allies, alumni, and professionals interested in supporting the vision.

Rights: Members have the right to attend meetings, access resources, vote, and participate in all programs.

Article 7

Elections

  • Elections held annually at end of each academic year
  • Candidates must be in good academic standing
  • Must be active in club activities
  • Voting by secret ballot
  • Results announced immediately after counting
Article 8

Meetings

  • General Meetings: At least once a month per campus
  • Executive Meetings: At least twice per semester
  • National Conference: Annually
  • Quorum: 50% + 1 of registered members
Article 9

Finance

  • Funded through dues, donations, sponsorships, and grants
  • Financial records must be transparent
  • Annual audits required
  • Two executive members must approve all transactions
Article 10

Amendments

Any proposed amendment must be submitted in writing, discussed at a general meeting, and approved by two-thirds of members present.

Article 11

Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, assets shall be transferred to another women-in-tech educational initiative, approved by a general vote.
